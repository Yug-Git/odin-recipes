Lasanga

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of wide, flat pasta sheets layered with a combination of rich meat sauce (often called ragù), creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and grated cheese like mozzarella and Parmesan. The layers are built in a baking dish and then baked until bubbly and golden on top.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 500g ground beef (or half beef, half pork)
  • 800g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

    • Steps

    1. Make the Meat Sauce

      2. Cook chopped onions and garlic in olive oil. Add ground meat and brown it. Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, herbs, salt, and pepper. Simmer until thick (about 30 minutes).

    2. Mix the Cheese Filling

      3. Combine ricotta cheese, one egg, salt, pepper, and grated Parmesan in a bowl. Mix until smooth and creamy.

    3. Boil the Lasagna Sheets (if needed)

      4. If not using oven-ready noodles, boil regular lasagna sheets until al dente. Drain and set aside.

    4. Assemble the Lasagna

      5. In a baking dish, layer in this order: sauce → noodles → ricotta mix → mozzarella → repeat. Finish with sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan on top.

    5. Bake

      6. Cover with foil and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 15–20 minutes until golden and bubbly. Let rest 10 minutes before serving.
